Smart Irrigation

Smart irrigation uses sensors, weather data, and automation to deliver water to plants based on their actual needs, conserving water and energy while promoting plant health and increasing crop yields. It automates traditional irrigation systems by monitoring real-time conditions, such as soil moisture and weather forecasts, to precisely adjust watering schedules and amounts, preventing overwatering and reducing waste from runoff and evaporation. Benefits include reduced water and energy consumption, lower operational costs, enhanced plant growth, improved resource management, and support for sustainable practices.

Sustainability

Sustainability in agriculture is a farming approach focused on producing food and fiber for the present without compromising future generations' ability to meet their own needs, by balancing environmental health, economic profitability, and social equity. Key practices include soil conservation, wise water management, pesticide reduction, and promoting biodiversity, all of which reduce environmental impact, increase ecosystem resilience, and improve the quality of life for farmers and communities. Agriculture is the world's largest industry. Intensive agriculture as it has been practiced since the 1960s has very high yields, but depletes the soil and pollutes the environment: greenhouse gas emissions, biodiversity loss, ocean acidification, etc.
